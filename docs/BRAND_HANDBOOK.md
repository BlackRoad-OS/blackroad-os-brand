# Blackroad Brand Handbook

## Voice & Tone
- **Precision with warmth.** Speak with technical confidence but pair it with welcoming invites.
- **Action-first.** Use verbs and short lines. Avoid filler and keep hierarchy tight.
- **Respect the traveler.** Accessibility, contrast, and motion safety take priority.

## Logo Usage
- Use `logo/blackroad-logo.svg` as the default wordmark. The monogram lives in `logo/blackroad-mark.svg`.
- Light contexts: prefer `*-dark.svg` for contrast on bright backgrounds.
- Dark contexts: prefer `*-light.svg` to maintain clarity against charcoal surfaces.
- Maintain clearspace equal to the height of the “B” around the mark. Minimum size: 24 px height for the mark, 56 px for the wordmark.

## Color Rules
- Accent colors are paired: pink for human spark, electric for system glow. Use together in gradients and pills.
- Neutral 900 / 50 define dark and light modes. Use overlays at 8–12% opacity for glassmorphism treatments.
- Never tint the logo outside of the provided tokens.

## Typography
- **Display:** Inter Tight — headlines, hero statements, and marquee numbers.
- **Body:** Inter — UI labels, documentation, and long-form copy.
- Track display text at +2%, body at +0%. Line heights: 1.1 for display, 1.5 for paragraphs.

## Icon Spacing
- Align icons to a 24 px grid. Add 2 px inset padding for enclosed shapes.
- Maintain consistent stroke weights; default is 1.5 px.

## Spiral Information Geometry
The Blackroad visual motif is a logarithmic spiral that represents continuous learning and expanding context. Use it in:
- **Backgrounds:** subtle radial gradients drifting from accent pink to electric teal.
- **Motion:** rotational easing that accelerates then coasts (see `logo/blackroad-spiral.lottie`).
- **Data viz:** arcing connectors and concentric annotations to signal relationships.

## Usage Examples
- **Buttons:** Primary buttons use accent pink background with electric hover; secondary buttons stay neutral with an outline.
- **Cards:** Dark glass panels with soft glows (`shadow-glow`) and rounded-xl corners.
- **Charts:** Use accent pink for primary series, electric for highlights, neutral tones for baselines.

## Don'ts
- Do not warp or skew the wordmark.
- Do not place the logo directly on photograph edges; always include neutral padding.
- Avoid long-running gradients that exceed 135°; keep them contained and intentional.
